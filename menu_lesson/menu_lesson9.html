A. Percakapan I

Di Warung Internet ( Warnet )

Billy came into a warnet (internet cafe) on Jalan W.R Supratman, Denpasar. The attendant of the warnet welcame Billy.
Petugas : Selamat siang.
    Good afternoon.
Billy : Selamat siang. Saya ingin mengirim e-mail. Komputer mana yang bisa saya pakai?
   Good afternoon. I want to send and e-mail. Which computer can i use?
Petugas : Komputer meja A dan G bisa di pakai. Pilih yang mana?
    The computer on desk A and G can be used. Which one will you choose?
Billy : Saya pilih meja A saja. Berapa biaya per jamnya ?
    I will choose the desk A. How much is it per hour?
Petugas : Rp. 5.000,00. per jam.
   Rp.5.000,00. per hour.
Billy : Bisa pakai disket sendiri?
    Can i use my own floppy disk?
Petugas : Ya, bisa.
   Yes, you can.
Billy : Apakah biayanya sama ?
   Is the price the same?
Petugas : ya, sama. Silahkan, komputernya sudah siap.
   Yes, it is. The computer is ready now.
Billy : Terima kasih.
    Thank you.
Petugas : Kalau perlu bantuan, panggil saya.
   If you need help, just call me.
Billy : Ya. Terima kasih.
   Yes. Thank you.
Catatan Kebahasaan
Close
(1) The word Warung in Indonesian means a place that sells food, drinks, and so on. Based on this term, in its later development, the word warung comes to be a place that provides telecommuncation service (a local and long distance call and fax services ) called warung tele-komunikasi), commonly it shortened as wartel. Recently, there appears warung internet, which is shortened as warnet.
(2) Rp stands for Rupiah. Rupiah is the currency of Indonesia..

A. Percakapan II

Di Kantor Polisi

Comming from the internet cafe, Billy arrived at Nyoman's home and he just realized that his wallet was not in his pocket. Besides name cards, there were also identity cards and a few credit cards. At the same time, a police called and asked him to come to his office. Billy went to the police station immediately.
Polisi : Selamat sore.
    Good afternoon.
Billy : Selamat sore. Baru saja ada polisi menelepon saya dan minta saya datang kesini.
   Good afternoon. A police had just called me and asked me to come here.
Polisi : Ya, saya yang menelepon anda. Anda pasti Billy Smith, bukan?
    Yes, i did call you. You must be Billy Smith, Right?
Billy : Benar, ada apa pak?
    I am. What the matter, Sir?
Polisi : Apa dompet anda hilang?
   Did you lose your wallet.
Billy : Betul. Darimana anda tahu?
    I did. How did you know?
Polisi : Begini, Bill. Tadi ada petugas warnet mengantar dompet ini kesini. Katanya, dompet ini tertinggal di warnet.
   Well, Bill. An internet cafe attendant has just sent this wallet here. he said, this wallet was found in the internet cafe.
Billy : Benarkah? aduh, saya pasti lupa.
   Really? i must had forgotten.
Polisi : Benar, Bill. Di dompet ini, ada kartu nama dan nomor telepon ponsel anda. Jadi saya pikir, ini dompet anda.
   Right, Bill. There are cards and your handphone number .
Billy : Sepertinya itu memang dompet saya, boleh saya melihatnya?
    It looks like my wallet, may i see it?
Polisi : Tentu. Ini dompetnya.
    sure. Here is the wallet.
Billy : Ini memang dompet saya. Tadi saya pikir dompet saya hilang. Saya baru saja akan melapor, Pak. Terima kasih.
    It is my wallet. I thought i had lost it. I was about to report it to you, Sir. Thank you.
Polisi : Terima kasih kembali. Saya ikut senang dompet anda tidak hilang
    You are welcome. I am glad you didn't lose your wallet.
Catatan Kebahasaan
Close
(1) Ponsel is the abbreviation of telephone selular, a mobile phone . In indonesia , ponsel is also called telepon genggam and HP. HP is abbreviation of handphone.
(2) In expressing feeling, the word aduh can be used to express some feelings, such as hurt feeling ( aduh, sakit sekali. "Oh, hurt." ),surprise ( aduh, ternyata orang itu berlari sangat cepat. "Gee, the person run very fast." ), happiness(aduh, senangnya melihat pemandangan itu. "Wow, it's so wonderful to see such a scenery." ), or upset (aduh, kenapa mereka membatalkan janji."Oh God, why do they break their promise." ).
Catatan Budaya
Close
(1) In Indonesia, inviting or offering actually has its characteristic. The invitation or offer that comes to us does not always need to be responded, to be accepted or to be rejected. The invitation or the offer is sometimes used for courtesy or hospitabilty. But in certain region in Indonesia, such as in Kutai, East Kalimantan, when someone asks or offers something, we have to receive it. It is taboo to reject an offer.

Tata Bahasa

1. Perbandingan

Qualifying words are words which give more specific description about something or someone being talked. These qualifying words can be combined with modifier, such as sangat"very", agak "rather", and cukup quite.
For example :

jauh → sangat jauh
far → very far		 dekat → sangat dekat
near → very near
Panas → agak panas
hot → rather hot		 dingin → agak dingin
cold → rather cold
pintar → cukup pintar
smart → quite smart		 gelap → cukup gelap
dark → quite dark

Further, the qualifying words can be used to make comparison in Indonesian. Comparison can be made by three levels, namely equal, comparative, and superlative.

a). Perbandingan tingkat setara or the equal level is used to describe the comparison between two people or objects that have specific similarity. They can be stated by two forms.

se- + adjective
or
sama + adjective + -nya + dengan

For example :

(1) Kamar Billy sebesar kamar Jane.
Kamar Billy sama besarnya dengan kamar Jane.
Billy's room is as bis as Jane's.
(2) Batik Solo semahal batik Pekalongan.
Batik Solo sama mahalnya dengan batik Pekalongan.
Batik Solo is as expensive as batik Pekalongan.
(3) kangkung sehijau bayam.
kangkung sama hijaunya dengan bayam.
Water spinach is as green as spinach.
(4) Coto Makasar seenak Soto Banjar.
Coto Makasar sama enaknya dengan Soto Banjar.
Makasar soup is as nice as Banjar soup
(5) Pantai Kuta seindah pantai Sanur.
Pantai Kuta sama indahnya dengan pantai Sanur.
Kuta beach is as beautiful as sanur beach.

b). The Comparative level is used to compare two people or objects that are not the same in a certain aspect. There are two ways in comparing objects, by using lebih "more" or kurang "less". This comparison can be made in the following way .

lebih + adjective + daripada
kurang + adjective + dibandingkan dengan

For example :

(1) Kamar Ani lebih besar daripada kamar Dini.
Ani's room is bigger than Dini's.
(2) Rini lebih cantik daripada wati.
Rini is prettier than wati.
(3) Kota Bandung lebih sejuk daripada kota Bogor.
Bandung is coller tahn Bogor.
(4) Sungai Barito kurang panjang dibandingkan dengan sungai kapuas.
Barito river is shorter (less long) than the Kapuas.
(5) Pohon pisang kurang tinggi dibandingkan dengan pohon kelapa.
Banana tress are shorter (less high) than coconut palms.

c). The Superlative level is used if there is somebody or something for a specific charáteristic or feature surpasses all other people or things in a group. This level is stated by using the word paling or the prefix ter- in a sentece.

paling + adjective
or
ter- + adjective

For example :

(1) Joko Paling pandai di kelasnya.
Joko terpandai di kelasnya.
Joko is the smartest in the class.
(2) Amin adalah anak paling kecil dirumahnya.
Amin adalah anak terkecil dirumahnya.
Amin is the smallest child in his family.
(3) Kuta adalah pantai paling indah di Bali.
Kuta adalah pantai terindah di Bali.
Kuta is the most beautiful beach in Bali.
(4) Berlian adalah batuan paling keras di dunia.
Berlian adalah batuan terkeras di dunia.
Diamonds are the hardest stones in the world.
(5) Supernova buku paling laris tahun 2002.
Supernova buku terlaris tahun 2002.
Supernova is the most popular book in 2002.

Look carefully at the example below.

(6) Amin lebih pandai daripada Bowo. Tetapi Lebih Joko lebih pandai daripada Amin. Jadi, Joko paling pandai.
Amin is smarter than Bowo, but Joko is smarter than Amin. So, Joko is the cleverest.

When there are more than two people of things being compared, the superlative form must be used.
