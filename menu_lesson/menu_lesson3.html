A. Percakapan I

Lapor Masuk Hotel Merdeka

Billy and Jane had arrieved at the hotel. They went straight to the reception desk where a nice receptionist welcame them
Resepsionis : Selamat siang.
   Good afternoon.
Billy dan Jane : Selamat siang.
   Ya.
Resepsionis : Ada yang dapat saya bantu, Pak?
    What can i do for you, Sir?
Billy : Masih ada kamar kosong?
   are there any rooms?
Resepsionis : Ya, anda. Ini daftar harga kamarnya.
    Yes, There are. Here is the room rate. Have a look, Sir.
Billy Baik, kami akan ambil dua kamar standar.
   All right, we will take two standar rooms
Resepsionis : Untuk berapa lama?
   How long would you like to stay, Sir?
Billy : Kami mau menginap tiga hari.
   We want to stay for three days
Resepsionis : Boleh lihat paspor Bapak?
   May i see your passport, Sir?
Billy : Tentu. Ini paspor saya.
   Sure. Here is my passport.
Resepsionis : Terima kasih. Anda akan bayar dengan tunai atau kartu kredit?
    Thank you. Would you like to pay cash or by credit card?
Billy : Kartu kredit. Ini kartu kredit saya.
   By credit card. Here is my credit card.
Resepsionis : Tunggu sebentar. Saya cek dulu.
   Wait s moment,i'll check it first
  Baik. Silakan, Ini kunci kamar bapak. Kamar 101 dan 102. Pelayan hotel akan mengantar anda ke sana.
   Well, Here are your keys,Sir. Room 101 and 102. The room boy will take you there
Billy : Terima kasih
   Thank you
Resepsionis : Selamat istirahat.
   Have a nice rest.
Catatan Kebahasaan
Close
(1) Kamar kosong means that the room is available to rent. It is not an empty room where there is nothing in it. Kamar kosong is used in colloquial Indonesian instead of the word kamar.

The next day, Billy and Jane wanted to go out for lunch. They would do some sight-seeing too. Before they left, they went to the reception desk and handed their room keys. to apply visa on arrival.
Resepsionis : Selamat siang.
   Good afternoon.
Billy : : Selamat siang, kami mau keluar untuk makan dan jalan-jalan.
   Good afternoon, we want to go out for lunch and do some sight-seeing
Jane : Ohya, saya mau tanya, dimana restoran yang bagus?
    By the way, where is good restaurant?
Resepsionis : Mmmm...Restoran Selera Indonesia saja. Masakannya lengkap dan enak.
   Well. The Selera Indonesia restaurant. The food is so various and delicious.
Jane : Apakah jauh dari sini?
    Is it far from here?
Resepsionis : Tidak juga. Dari sini, ke arah kiri. Nanti di perempatan, Anda belok kanan. Restoran itu kits-kira 100 meter dari situ. Letaknya di sebelah rumah sakit Bahagia.
    Not really. Take the left from here. On the crossroads, turn the right. The restaurant is 100 meters away from there, next to Bahagia hospital.
Resepsionis : Kembali
   You are welcome.

Close
(1) The word Jalan-jalan may mean streets and sight-seeing . The use of it depends on the context given.

Tata Bahasa

1. Saya, Anda dan Dia

Saya, Anda and Dia are used when the speaker,the listener, and the one being talked about is singular in number. for example :

Billy : Saya Billy ,Anda siapa ?
Nyoman : Saya Nyoman
Billy : Saya dari Australia , anda darimana?
Nyoman : Saya dari Indonesia

As a speaker, Billy refers himself as saya and he refers his listener, Nyoman , as anda. In daily informal situation, saya is sometimes replaced by aku, anda replaced by kamu.
For the example :


Billy : Aku Billy, Kamu siapa ?
Nyoman : Aku Nyoman.
Billy : Aku dari Australia, Kamu darimana?
Nyoman : Aku dari Indonesia.

Saya, aku, Anda and Kamu can used when to form possesiveness.
For example :

(1) Billy Teman saya .
Billy Temanku.
Billy is my friend.
(2) Kamar Anda nomor 135.
kamarmu nomor 135.
Your room is number 135.

From the examples, it can be seen that in order to form possesiveness Saya and anda are used in their full forms, while aku and Kamu are used in their short forms, -ku and -mu.

Billy : Saya Billy dan dan dia Jane

As a speaker, Billy refers himself as Saya and he refers Jane as dia because jane is a person that he is being talked about. Similiar to saya or anda,dia can also be used to state possesiveness. In this case dia changes into -nya.
For example :

(3) Ini kamar dia → ini kamarnya
(4) Itu paspor dia → itu paspornya

2. Kata Tanya di mana, ke mana , dan dari mana

In Indonesia, the information of place or a location can be formed using di, ke,or dari before the words that indicate place or location. di states "Where something or someone is", ke states "where something or someone is heading for", dari states "where something or someone is from".
For example :

di rumah at home
di kantor at office
di hotel at hotel

ke kanan to the right
ke Bandung to Bandung
ke pasar to the market
dari Bali from Bali
dari jepang from Japan
di Perancis from France

(1) Billy dan Jane menginap di Hotel Merdeka
Billy dan Jane stayed at Hotel Merdeka.
(2) Billy ke Bandung bulan lalu.
Billy went to Bandung last month.
(3) Mereka datang dari Jepang.
They come from Japan.

further,question word to ask a place or location can be formed by combining di, ke and dari with mana to form di mana, ke mana and dari mana.

The question word di mana (where at) is used to ask about the location or the existance of something or someone.
For Example :

(1) Anda tinggal di mana? .
Where do u live?.		 Saya tinggal di Jakarta?
I live in Jakarta.
(2) Di mana kopor anda?
Where is your suitcase?.		 kopor saya ada di kamar.
My suitcase is in the room.
(3) Di mana letak telepon umum ? .
Where is the public telephone?.		 Telepon umum ada di Jalan Wahid.
It is on Jalan Wahid.

The question word ke mana (where to) is used to ask about the direction or aim of something or someone.
For Example :

(4) Anda mau ke mana? .
Where do u go?.		 Saya mau ke Bogor?
I am going to Bogor.
(5) Besok kita pergi ke mana ?
Where will we go tomorrow?.		 Besok kita pergi ke Bali.
Tomorrow we will go to bali.
(6) ke mana Billy pergi? .
Where did billy go?.		 Billy pergi ke pasar.
Billy went to the market.

The question word dari mana (where from) is used to ask about where something or someone is from.
For Example :

(7) Dari mana Ali? .
Where is ali from?		 Ali dari hotel?
Ali is from the hotel.
(5) Anda mendapat buku itu dari mana ?
Where did u get the book from?.		 dari perpustakaan.
From the library.
(6) Dari mana orang-orang itu? .
Where do those people come from?.		 mereka dari jawa.
They come from java.
