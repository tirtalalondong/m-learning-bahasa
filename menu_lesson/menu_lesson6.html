A. Percakapan I

Di Anjungan Sulawesi Selatan

On sunday morning, Billy and Jane were making a trip to Taman Mini Indonesia Indah, a tourist spot in the east of Jakarta. They were going to visit several pavillions such as the pavillion of South sulawesi and the pavillion of Yogyakarta. When they were at pavillion of South sulawesi, they were accompanied by a guide.
Jane : Wah megah sekali rumah ini.
   Wow, it is very fantastic. What house is this ?
Pemandu : Ini Tongkonan, rumah adat Toraja di Sulawesi Selatan.
   This is Tongkonan, a traditional house of Toraja in the south of Sulawesi.
Jane : Unik ya. Atapnya seperti perahu.
    Unique, isn't it? The roof is like a boat.
Pemandu : Ya . Tiangnya juga dibuat dari kayu yang kokoh.
   It is. The pillars are made of strong wood too.
Billy : Ukirannya indah sekali.
    Its carving is so beautiful too.
Pemandu : Ya. Anda bisa lihat ukiran seperti kepala kerbau di dinding itu.
   Yes. look at the buffalo's head carved on that wall.
Billy : Wah, hebat sekali.
   Wow, it is fantastic.
Pemandu : Ya, memang luar biasa. Oh ya, sebaiknya anda melihat rumah adat yang lain karena semua unik.
   It is fantastic. By the way, you must see the traditional houses because they are all unique
Jane : Baiklah.
   Sure.
Pemandu : Kalau begitu, mari kita berkeliling.
   Well, let's go around then .

(1) Rumah adat is a house which shows special characteristics of Indonesian tribes.

A. Percakapan II

Di Anjungan Daerah Istimewa Yogyakarta

After admiring Tongkonan house at the pavillion of South Sulawesi. Billy and Jane stopped by at the Yogyakarta. A guide welcame the by the gate hospitably.
Pemandu : Selamat datang di Anjungan Yogyakarta.
    Welcome to Yogyakarta pavillion.
Billy : Terima kasih.
   Thank you.
Jane : Hei. Suara apa itu?
    Listen, What sound is that?
Pemandu : Oh, itu suara gamelan.
   That is the sound of gamelan.
Billy : Ayo kita lihat kesana.
    Let's go there and see it.
Jane : Wah, ada tari. Tari apa itu?
    Wow, there is a dance. What dance is it
Pemandu : Itu tari Gambyong, tari traditional from Yogyakarta.
    That is Gambyong dance, a traditional dance from Yogyakarta.
Billy : apakah setiap hari ada pertunjukan tari?
   Is there any dance performance everyday?
Pemandu : Tidak. Hanya ada pada hari minggu.
    No, there is not. it is only performed on sunday.
Billy : Di anjungan lain ada tari seperti ini?
   Is there any similiar dance at the other pavillions?
Pemandu : Ya ada. Kalau mau. Anda bisa mengunjungi anjungan Bali. Sekarang disana ada tari Pendet.
    Yes, there is. If you like, you may visit the pavillion of Bali. Pendet dance is being performed there..
Billy : Baiklah.
    All right.

A. Percakapan III

Di Anjungan Bali

Billy and Jane arrived at the pavillion of Bali, Taman Mini Indonesia Indah.
Pemandu : Nah. Itu tari Pendet, tari selamat datang.

Catatan Budaya
Close
(1) Taman Mini Indonesia Indah (TMII) is a popular tourist destination which is located in East Jakarta. According its name, taman means "garden" and mini is means "small" so Taman Mini Indonesia Indah means Indonsia in its small form or the miniature of Indonesia. As the miniature of Indonesia. it is completed with traditional house and arts from all over places in Indonesia. At TMII there are also some museums and recreations parks.
(2) The traditional performance which are usually presented in TMII are Reog and kuda lumping. Reog is a traditional performance from East Java. Reog has a magical element and played by a number of men wearing masks. The main dancer wears a lion head decorated with peacock feathers. The most famous Reog is from the region Ponorogo, and so it is often called Reog Ponorogo. Meanwhile, Kuda lumping is also a name of another magical traditional performance played only by men. kuda lumping is a horse like model made of feather or plaited bamboo. It is commonly played as a part of Reog perfomance and sometimes it is performance individually.
(3) Gamelan is a Javanse and Balinese traditional percussion orchestra comprised with brass instrument, such as the saron, bonang, gambang,gendang, gong, and rebab. The instruments are played by hitting them with a soft "hammer" except for the rebab (a two stringed instrument) Which is played like a celo.
(4) Pukul and jam are two words wich can be used to ask or to inform hours in Indonesian. Pukul is used in formal situation. Although both Inform hours, it is only jam which can be indication duration.
(5) In Indonesia, it is acceptable to ask about numbers of children to a married person or to ask about age to someone.

Tata Bahasa

1. Kata tanya Kapan

There are some words that can be used to inform time in indonesian. For example :

(1) Billy sedang makan siang sekarang .
Billy is having his lunch now.
(2) Kami akan pergi ke Bogor Besok.
We will go to Bogor tomorrow.
(3) Kami pergi ke Bogor kemarin .
We went to Bogor yesterday.
(4) Tahun depan Jane akan bekerja di Bali.
Next year Jane will work in Bali.
(5) Dua tahun yang Jane bekerja di Bali
Two years a go Jane worked in Bali.

Further the question word that is used to ask time in Indonesian is kapan. This question can be placed at the begining or at the end of sentence.
For example :

(6) Kapan Paman datang?
When did uncle come?		 Paman datang kemarin.
Uncle come yesterday.
(7) Paman datang Kapan?
When will uncle come?		 Paman datang besok.
Uncle will come tomorrow.
(8) Kapan kita pergi?
When will we go?		 Besok kita pergi.
Tomorrow we will go.

In the informal situation, the answer to those question may be short. They do not have to be in the full grammatical form.
For example :

(8 Kapan Paman datang?
When did uncle come?		 Kemarin.
Yesterday.
(10) Paman datang Kapan?
When will uncle come?		 Besok.
Tomorrow.
(11) Kapan kita pergi?
When will we go?		 Besok .
Tomorrow.

2. Ajakan

Ajakan is invitation in Indonesian expects a response from the listener to join what is being done (or going to be done) by the speaker or to do what is being done (or going to be done) by the speaker or to do what is suggested by the speaker. Word that is usually used to invited is mari or ayo , which interchangeable.
For example :

(1) Mari kita belajar bahasa Indonesia.
let's learn Indonesian.
(2) Mari kita pergi ke Taman Mini.
let's go to Taman Mini.
(3) Mari kita lestarikan lingkungan kita.
let's preserve our environment.
(4) Ayo kita pulang.
let's go home.
(5) Ayo kita makan bersama-sama.
let's learn eat together.

Mari tends to be used in formal communication, whereas ayo is used more informal communication ayo is sometimes abbreviated to yo, which can be used at the end of the sentece. The abbreviated form yo does not have the particle -lah added.
For example :

(6) Besok kita ke Puncak , yo.
let's go to Puncak tomorrow.
(7) Kita minum kopi dulu, yo.
let's have coffe.

Besides using lama,the question word berapa can also be combined witd word which indicates time, such as hour, day , month or year.
For example :

(10) Berapa tahunanda belajar bahasa Indonesia?
How many years have you studied Indonesian?		 saya belajar bahasa Indonesia selama dua tahun.
I have studied Indonesian for two years.
(11) Berapa harianda tinggal di Jakarta?
How long did you stay in Jakarta?		 Saya tinggal di Jakarta selama tiga hari.
I stayed in Jakarta for three days.

The particle -lah can also be added to mari and ayo become marilah and ayolah. As well as making it more polite , the addition of the particle -lah makes the invitation a little more sincere. So, the word marilah can also be used to convince the listener(s).
For example :

(8) Mari kita jaga lingkungan kita.
let's look after our environment.
(9) Marilah kita jaga lingkungan kita.
let's look after our environment.
(10) Mari kita beristirahat.
let's take a rest.
(11) Marilah kita beristirat.
let's take a rest.

The addition of the particle -lah to ayo not only expresses sincerity, but also makes the invitation firmer. so if ayo does not convince the listener enough, the form ayolah can be used to emphasise or express the sincerity of the speaker.
For example :

(12a) Ayo kita beramai-ramai ke Bali. (12b) Ayolah kita beramai-ramai ke Bali.
let's go to Bali together.
(13a) Ayo kesini. Jangan malu-malu. (13b) Ayolah kesini. Jangan malu-malu.
Don't be shy.
